# The Children Still Needed Breakfast

One of the strangest things about trauma is that ordinary life continues while your nervous system is falling apart.

The children still needed breakfast.

School uniforms still needed washing.
Shoes still needed finding.
Homework still existed.
People still expected replies.
Appointments still happened.
The world kept moving normally while internally I felt like I was drowning.

That is the part people do not always see.

Trauma is not always dramatic on the outside.

Sometimes it is standing in the kitchen making toast while your chest feels tight with fear.

Sometimes it is packing lunchboxes while trying not to cry.

Sometimes it is smiling at your children because they deserve softness even when your nervous system feels shattered.

I think many traumatised parents become experts at functioning while emotionally overwhelmed.

Not because they are “fine”.

Because the children still need comfort.
The children still need routines.
The children still need safety.
The children still need someone to keep going.

So you keep going.

Even when your body is exhausted.
Even when your brain feels overloaded.
Even when fear has been sitting inside your nervous system for far too long.

People often ask traumatised parents:
“How are you coping?”

But I think the more honest question is:
“How did you keep carrying so much while still trying to love your children gently?”

Because many parents are surviving invisible emotional battles while still trying to create ordinary childhood moments for the people they love most.

And sometimes that survival goes completely unseen.
