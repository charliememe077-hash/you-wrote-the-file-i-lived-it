# When Autism Meets Trauma

People often misunderstand what happens when autism and trauma collide inside the same nervous system.

They see overwhelm.
They see shutdowns.
They see panic, hypervigilance, exhaustion, confusion, emotional intensity, sensory distress, or difficulty regulating.

But they do not always see the years of survival underneath it.

Autistic people often experience the world with heightened sensitivity long before trauma ever enters the picture.

Then trauma teaches the body that nowhere is fully safe.

The result is not attention-seeking.
It is not manipulation.
It is not “bad behaviour.”

It is a nervous system carrying too much for too long.

Sometimes the autistic person is trying to:
- process fear
- survive uncertainty
- understand conflicting information
- stay emotionally regulated
- prevent rejection
- maintain safety
- hold themselves together in environments that feel unpredictable

All while still trying to appear “normal” enough for other people to stay comfortable.
