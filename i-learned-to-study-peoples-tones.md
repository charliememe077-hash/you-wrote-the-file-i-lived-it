# I Learned To Study People's Tones

Before I understood trauma,
before I understood autism,
before I understood hypervigilance,

I learned to study people.

Their tone.
Their facial expressions.
Their pauses.
Their body language.
The atmosphere in a room.

I learned to scan for danger before I even realised I was doing it.

A small change in someone’s voice could completely shift my nervous system.

One delayed reply.
One sigh.
One look.
One cold sentence.

And suddenly my body would prepare for rejection, conflict, abandonment, criticism, or emotional withdrawal.

People often think hypervigilance is dramatic.

But sometimes it is incredibly quiet.

Sometimes it looks like:
- overthinking
- people pleasing
- emotional exhaustion
- analysing conversations afterwards
- rehearsing responses
- masking panic behind politeness
- trying desperately not to upset anyone

The nervous system learns these patterns for a reason.

Especially after trauma.

Especially in autistic people who are already processing the world intensely.

You become deeply aware of emotional shifts around you because your body is trying to keep you safe.

But living like that is exhausting.

Because eventually you are no longer just listening to words.

You are listening for danger hidden inside them.
