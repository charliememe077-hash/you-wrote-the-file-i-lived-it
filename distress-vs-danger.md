# Distress vs Danger

One of the most damaging things systems can do is confuse distress with danger.

Distress can look loud.
Fear can look chaotic.
Trauma can look defensive.
Autistic overwhelm can look reactive.

But none of those things automatically mean someone is dangerous.

Sometimes what professionals are actually witnessing is a nervous system in survival mode.

The difference matters.

Because when distress is repeatedly recorded as risk, the file can slowly move further away from the human being living inside it.
