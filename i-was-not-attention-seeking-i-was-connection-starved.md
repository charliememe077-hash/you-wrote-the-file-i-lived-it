# I Was Not Attention-Seeking. I Was Connection Starved.

One of the most painful things I have realised is how often unmet human needs get misunderstood.

Especially in traumatised people.

Especially in autistic people.

Especially in people who spent years surviving emotionally unsafe environments.

I was not attention-seeking.

I was connection starved.

There is a difference.

Attention-seeking implies performance.
Manipulation.
Drama.
Wanting to be watched.

But connection starvation feels completely different inside the body.

It feels like your nervous system has spent years searching for emotional safety it never fully learned how to trust.

It feels like becoming emotionally overwhelmed by small moments of gentleness because your body is not used to feeling emotionally safe with people.

It feels confusing when calmness affects you deeply.

It feels frightening when kindness reaches parts of you that survival mode kept hidden for years.

I think many traumatised people carry enormous unmet attachment needs without fully understanding them.

Not because they are childish.

Not because they are manipulative.

Because human beings are not designed to survive prolonged emotional fear without connection.

People need emotional safety.
People need co-regulation.
People need warmth.
People need to feel emotionally held sometimes.

And when those needs go unmet for long enough, eventually the nervous system starts responding intensely to moments of safety, gentleness or emotional attunement.

Especially if those experiences were rare.

I think systems and society sometimes pathologise attachment needs instead of understanding the deprivation underneath them.

Especially in adults.

Especially in women.

Especially in traumatised people who learned to survive without consistent emotional safety.

The truth is:
I was not trying to be “too much.”

My nervous system was exhausted from carrying too much alone.

And sometimes what looks like emotional intensity is actually a human being starving quietly for safety, softness, understanding and connection.
