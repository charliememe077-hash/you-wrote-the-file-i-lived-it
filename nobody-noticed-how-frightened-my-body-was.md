# Nobody Noticed How Frightened My Body Was

Nobody noticed how frightened my body was because I still looked functional.

That is the thing I keep coming back to.

I could still speak properly.
Still attend meetings.
Still answer questions.
Still make phone calls.
Still get the children to school.
Still hold conversations.
Still try to appear reasonable.

So people assumed I was coping.

But my nervous system was living like danger could happen at any moment.

I do not think people always understand what prolonged fear does to the body.

Fear changes the way you hear voices.
The way you read facial expressions.
The way your stomach tightens when somebody’s tone changes slightly.
The way your chest feels when you think you have upset somebody.
The way your body panics when you feel misunderstood.

Trauma teaches the nervous system to constantly scan for danger.

And when that fear lasts for years, eventually your body forgets how to fully relax.

Even calmness can start feeling unfamiliar.

I became extremely good at surviving while frightened.

That is why so many people missed what was actually happening internally.

Because survival mode can still look functional from the outside.

Especially in mothers.
Especially in autistic women.
Especially in people who learned very young that emotions had to be hidden to stay safe.

People often think trauma is only visible when somebody is completely falling apart.

But sometimes trauma looks like:
smiling politely while internally panicking.

Sometimes trauma looks like:
overexplaining because your body is terrified of being misunderstood.

Sometimes trauma looks like:
appearing emotionally intense because your nervous system has spent too long trapped in survival mode.

I think many people are walking around carrying invisible fear inside bodies that never truly learned safety.

And I think systems often underestimate how much emotional pain a human being can hide behind “functioning”.

Because functioning is not the same thing as feeling safe.

Sometimes functioning is simply survival wearing a socially acceptable mask.
