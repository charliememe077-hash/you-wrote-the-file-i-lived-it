# The Gap

There is often a gap between:

- what professionals record
- and what human beings actually experience

A gap between:
- behaviour and fear
- compliance and safety
- appearing functional and actually coping
- procedural language and nervous-system reality

Inside systems, repeated wording can slowly become accepted as fact.

Interpretations can harden over time.

Distress can become reframed as danger.
Fear can become reframed as instability.
Attachment can become reframed as dependency.
Autistic overwhelm can become reframed as emotional dysregulation without context.

And eventually the file may begin speaking louder than the human being living inside it.

This project exists inside that gap.

Not to reject safeguarding.

But to ask harder questions about:
- interpretation
- proportionality
- trauma awareness
- nervous-system literacy
- autism understanding
- and the emotional consequences of prolonged professional scrutiny

Because what gets written down matters.

But so does what gets missed.
