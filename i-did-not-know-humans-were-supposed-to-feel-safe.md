# I Did Not Know Humans Were Supposed to Feel Safe

One of the most painful things I have realised is that I spent most of my life thinking survival was normal.

I thought hypervigilance was personality.
I thought exhaustion was weakness.
I thought overexplaining was just who I was.
I thought being constantly emotionally alert was normal adulthood.

I did not realise other people moved through life without constantly scanning for danger.

I did not realise some people wake up without fear already sitting in their chest.

I did not realise safety was something the nervous system was actually supposed to feel.

For years my body stayed prepared for things that were not even happening anymore.

That is what prolonged trauma can do.

Your body keeps living like the danger never fully ended.

Even in quiet moments.
Even during normal days.
Even when your children are laughing.
Even when nothing bad is happening.

Part of you still waits for something to go wrong.

I became very good at functioning while frightened.

Very good at masking.
Very good at surviving.
Very good at appearing “capable” while internally exhausted.

And because I could still function, many people never realised how overwhelmed my nervous system actually was.

That is the strange thing about survival mode.

Sometimes the people struggling the most still look functional from the outside.

I think that is why trauma can be so invisible.

Especially in adults.
Especially in mothers.
Especially in autistic people who spent years learning to hide overwhelm just to survive socially.

Nobody teaches you how deeply fear can settle into the body.

Nobody teaches you what happens when your nervous system never truly learns what safety feels like.

And nobody prepares you for how emotional it feels once you finally begin to realise you deserved safety all along.
