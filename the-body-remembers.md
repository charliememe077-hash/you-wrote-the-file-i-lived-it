# The Body Remembers What the File Never Recorded

Files record behaviour.

They record timelines.
Words.
Meetings.
Observations.
Incidents.
Professional interpretations.

But the body records something else entirely.

The body records fear.

It remembers the sound of footsteps.
The change in someone’s tone.
The feeling of uncertainty sitting in a room.
The panic underneath waiting.
The exhaustion of never fully feeling safe.

A file might describe someone as emotional, reactive, defensive, resistant, chaotic, intense, or dysregulated.

But the body knows what survival felt like underneath those words.

Sometimes people are not reacting to the present moment alone.

Sometimes their nervous system is reacting to accumulated fear, past experiences, attachment wounds, trauma, sensory overwhelm, and the unbearable weight of prolonged uncertainty.

Especially when someone is autistic.

Especially when they have spent years trying to hold themselves together while still appearing functional enough for systems to remain comfortable around them.

The file may record the behaviour.

But the body remembers the cost.
