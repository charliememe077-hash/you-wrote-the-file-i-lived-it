# About

"You Wrote the File. I Lived It." is a lived experience writing archive exploring:

- Autism
- Trauma
- Safeguarding
- Nervous-system survival
- Distress vs danger
- Attachment
- Hypervigilance
- The gap between procedural systems and emotional reality

This project was created to explore the difference between what gets formally recorded and what it actually feels like to live through systems as a human being.

Much of this writing focuses on how fear, trauma, autistic overwhelm, attachment wounds, and survival responses can become misunderstood inside professional systems.

The aim is not to attack safeguarding.

The aim is accuracy.
Humanity.
Reflection.
And a deeper understanding of the nervous system underneath behaviour.

Because files can record events.

But they do not always record the body carrying them.
