# When The File Becomes The Story

One of the hardest things about systems is how quickly repetition can create certainty.

A concern gets written down once.

Then repeated.
Then summarised.
Then quoted.
Then copied into another assessment.
Then referenced again later.

Over time, interpretation can begin to feel indistinguishable from fact.

Especially when the original emotional context disappears.

Especially when fear is observed externally rather than understood internally.

The human being may still be trying to explain:
- what they meant
- what they felt
- what triggered the reaction
- what the body was responding to
- what history existed underneath the moment

But the system may already be responding to the accumulated weight of documentation rather than the complexity of the person.

That is part of the gap too.

Not maliciousness.

Not necessarily cruelty.

But drift.

The slow distance that can form between:
- lived experience
- professional interpretation
- and recorded narrative

And once that distance exists, it can become incredibly difficult for the person inside the file to feel fully seen again.

Especially when they are already traumatised.

Especially when they are autistic.

Especially when they are trying to stay regulated while being observed, assessed, interpreted, and repeatedly discussed inside systems they cannot fully control.

The body feels all of that.

Even when the paperwork does not.
